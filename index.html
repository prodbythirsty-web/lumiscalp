The Science

How red light actually works

Not magic. Not placebo. Photobiomodulation is one of the most studied non-invasive approaches to scalp health, with the mechanism understood at the cellular level.

660nm

Red Light Wavelength

At 660 nanometers, red light sits right in the window where follicle cells absorb it most efficiently. When that light hits the mitochondria inside a follicle cell, it triggers a cascade: more ATP is produced, which is the cell's primary energy currency. More energy means better-functioning follicles, which is exactly the environment your hair needs to thrive. This is the same wavelength studied extensively in peer-reviewed photobiomodulation research.

850nm

Near-Infrared Depth

While 660nm red light works at the surface, 850nm near-infrared light penetrates 4–5mm into scalp tissue — deep enough to reach the follicle base and the capillary network around it. This supports circulation at the root level, which is how nutrients and oxygen actually reach follicles in the first place. Poor scalp circulation is one of the most underappreciated contributors to thinning hair, and near-infrared directly addresses it.

25min

Built Into Your Day

The reason most people fail with hair treatments is compliance. Pills you forget, serums that feel gross, routines that require 40 minutes of focused effort. LumiScalp is different: you put it on while you drink your morning coffee, watch TV, take a Zoom call. 25 minutes, completely hands-free, and you're done. The cap covers your full scalp simultaneously — no need to move it around or think about coverage.
